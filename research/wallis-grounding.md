## Who Parādevī is

> "Para Devi... is the lineage goddess of the Trika lineage."
> — as taught by Christopher Wallis, office-hours AMA satsang (`youtube/0000-00-00-office-hours-ask-me-anything.md`, ~19 min)

> "Para Devi though, like I said, is a multivalent deity."
> — same session

> "...when we say svatma rupa, the nature of one's own self, and when we say Paradevi... we're talking actually ultimately about the same thing."
> — Wallis, Vijñāna Bhairava Tantra verse 76 session (`youtube/0000-00-00-vij-na-bhairava-tantra-verse-76-open-eyed-meditation-on-dapp.md`)

> "It said the supreme goddess Paradevi can manifest in that state."
> — same session (on the liminal state between waking and sleeping, VBT vv. 75-76)

From Wallis's published Tantrasāra translation (Abhinavagupta, ch. 4), Parā as the Transcendent Power:

> "...as pure undifferentiated Awareness is its sacred Transcendent Power, Parā-śakti."
> — Wallis (trans.), blog 2019-07-31 (`blog/2019-07-31-the-aspects-of-the-goddess-tantrasara-chapter-four-part-4.md`)

> "In this system, the Highest Divinity has as its essence all-inclusive expanded Awareness."
> — same translation

So: Parādevī is the supreme Goddess of the Trika, ultimately identical with the nature of one's own Self — Awareness itself. The "light of Consciousness" (prakāśa) framing appears in material Wallis hosts:

> "...expert in spinning the wheel of the supreme sun, the rays of [the light of Consciousness:] prakāśa."
> — Swāmī Lakṣman-jū's commentary on the 12th Kālī, quoted in Wallis's blog (`blog/2023-02-18-the-twelve-kalis-part-three.md`)

## Who Kālī is

> "Materials for the contemplation of the Twelve Kālīs, the most important and most 'secret' doctrine of the Krama lineage."
> — Wallis, blog 2023-02-05 (`blog/2023-02-05-the-twelve-kalis-part-one.md`)

In the Tantrasāra translation, the names of the one Power include:

> "...the Black One (Kālī), She Who Devours Time (Kālī Saṅkarṣiṇī)..."
> — Wallis (trans.), blog 2019-07-31

And crucially, the devouring/transcendent aspect is named as Parā herself:

> "She by whose power it devours this threefold process... is simply the Blessed Goddess Śrī Parā."
> — Wallis (trans.), blog 2019-07-31 (the alternate names given there include "Kālakarṣiṇī ('She Who Devours Time', aka Kālī)")

The Twelve Kālīs are the cycle of every act of perception (creation, persistence, dissolution, and the nameless ground, applied across object, means, and knower) — Wallis presents Abhinavagupta's full sequence in the 2019 post and the three "Twelve Kalis" posts (2023-02-05, -09, -18).

## How they relate — Wallis's actual framing

The builder's working phrase was "one is the emanation of the other." The corpus gives a more precise (and more interesting) picture:

> "...you know, Kali and Para are two sides of the same coin. They're aspects of each other."
> — Wallis, AMA satsang, answering a question on the relationship of Kālī and Parā Vāk / Parā Devī (`youtube/0000-00-00-ask-me-anything-satsang-from-fb-group.md`, ~1:09, video uEScKMiMruM, t=4140s; context: "for the Kaula Trika lineage of Abhinavagupta, which is Krama influenced")

And then, astonishingly for this project:

> "Kali is the the black hole, if you will, and [Parā] is the white hole."
> — same session, t=4170s. **Transcript garble flag:** the auto-caption reads "prana is the white hole," but the question and preceding sentence are about Kali and Parā; "Parā" is the near-certain reading. He immediately notes the physics metaphor is dated (physicists have largely dropped paired white holes) — he offers it as image, not cosmology. VERIFY against the video before quoting publicly.

Emanation language does appear — but it describes the internal cycle of Kālī herself, not a hierarchy between Kālī and Parā:

> "So, the 12 Kalis are emanations of Mahakali."
> — same session, t=4050s

> "But the 13th Kali is not separate from the 12. She emanates as the 12... she subsumes the 12."
> — same session, t=4080s

> "So the first expansion of Parā Devī, the description of sṛṣṭi-daśā within prameya."
> — Swāmī Lakṣman-jū's commentary on Sṛṣṭikālī, quoted in Wallis's blog (`blog/2023-02-05-the-twelve-kalis-part-one.md`) — the Twelve Kālīs as Parā Devī's own expansion

## Synthesis (doctrinal anchor for this project)

As taught by Christopher Wallis in the sources above:

1. **Parādevī** is the supreme Goddess of the Trika lineage — pure undifferentiated Awareness, ultimately the nature of one's own Self.
2. **Kālī** (Kālasaṃkarṣiṇī, "She Who Devours Time") is the dissolving, time-devouring, transcendent movement of that same Awareness — the heart of the Krama lineage's Twelve Kālīs cycle.
3. **The relationship is mutual aspecthood, not one-way emanation.** In the Kaula Trika synthesis of Abhinavagupta, Kālī and Parā are "two sides of the same coin... aspects of each other"; in the Tantrasāra, the devouring power "is simply the Blessed Goddess Śrī Parā" under another name. Emanation is what the Goddess does *as her own cycle* (Mahākālī emanating as, and subsuming, the Twelve).
4. **Design correction adopted:** the project's `emanation` cross-reference between Kali and Paradevi items is therefore **bidirectional** — a flip, not a descent. See `DESIGN.md` section 1.
5. **The black-hole image is Wallis's own** (with the garble caveat above): he reached for exactly this metaphor for Kālī, in public teaching, unprompted by this project. The system's central visual conceit has a source in the teaching it honors — while remaining, like his usage, an image rather than a physics claim.
